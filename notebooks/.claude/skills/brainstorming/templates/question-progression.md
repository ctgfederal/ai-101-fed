# Question Progression

The 8-question order. Adapt to user energy — skip what they've answered, dig deeper where they're excited.

| # | Category | Example phrasing | What you're listening for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Inspiration | "What's inspiring this? What triggered the idea?" | The motivation. Why now. |
| 2 | Projects | "What specific projects or products are you imagining?" | Concreteness. Forms it could take. |
| 3 | Audience | "Who is this for? Who cares about this?" | Personas. Their pain. |
| 4 | Use Cases | "How will people actually use this?" | Real scenarios, not aspirations. |
| 5 | Desired Outcomes | "What does success look like? What changes when this exists?" | Observable changes, not features. |
| 6 | Guiding Principles | "What matters most — speed, quality, simplicity, flexibility, etc.?" | Tradeoffs they'd accept. |
| 7 | Constraints | "What constraints exist (tech, time, budget, compliance)?" | Hard limits. |
| 8 | Scope | "What is explicitly NOT included?" | What stays out. |

## Stop conditions
- Vision is clear (you can summarize it back accurately).
- User says "proceed", "I'm done", or asks for next step.
- User repeatedly answers "I don't know" — capture that as an open question and stop.
